01 — Context

A compliance platform with high-stakes, multi-client complexity

JJ Keller & Associates is a leading provider of regulatory compliance solutions — serving businesses across transportation, workplace safety, and HR. Their platform handles complex workflows for a diverse range of clients, each with different regulatory needs, user roles, and data requirements.

The redesign was not just a visual refresh. It involved restructuring navigation, rethinking information architecture, and updating interaction patterns across deeply interconnected compliance flows — all while serving multiple client types simultaneously.

💡 In a compliance platform, a broken user flow isn't just bad UX — it's a risk event. Users who can't complete a regulatory process on time face real business consequences.

03 — Problem Discovery

Design inconsistencies and broken flows in the redesign

🗺️
Navigation
Redesigned nav breaking user paths

New navigation patterns disrupted established user flows — users couldn't reach key sections through the expected paths after the redesign.

📋
Forms & Flows
Broken steps in compliance workflows

Multi-step forms and submission flows failed at specific steps — preventing users from completing regulatory processes end-to-end.

🎨
Design Fidelity
Figma spec inconsistencies

Typography, spacing, and component styles deviated from the redesign specs — creating visual inconsistency across the platform.

📱
Responsive
Layout failures on smaller screens

Responsive behavior broke at mobile and tablet viewports, particularly in data-dense compliance views designed primarily for desktop.

04 — Process

Systematic testing across flows, clients, and breakpoints

01
Mapped the full flow matrix before testing

I started by understanding the scope: which flows existed, how they varied by client type and user role, and which were highest-risk from a compliance standpoint. This informed a test priority framework.

02
End-to-end flow testing across client configurations

I walked through each compliance workflow from start to finish across different client contexts, documenting where flows broke, stalled, or produced unexpected outcomes.

03
Figma validation on redesigned components

In parallel, I compared the redesigned UI components against Figma specs, flagging typography, spacing, and interaction deviations across the platform.

04
Cross-device responsive testing

Given the platform's complexity, I specifically tested data-heavy views on mobile and tablet — surfaces where compliance users increasingly need access on the go.

05
Prioritized bugs by user and business impact

With many issues found, I triaged by severity — prioritizing anything that blocked a compliance workflow completion over cosmetic or minor UX issues — and documented everything in JIRA with reproduction steps.

06
Collaborated with dev team through fixes and regression

I worked closely with engineers throughout the fix cycle — clarifying issue context, validating fixes, and running full regression passes to ensure no new issues were introduced.

06 — Product Thinking

What complex flow testing taught me about product

JJ Keller pushed me to think about product quality at a systems level. When flows are this interconnected, a bug in one place can cascade through the entire experience. Understanding the full map of user journeys — not just individual screens — is what made testing effective.

This kind of work is deeply aligned with how a Product Manager needs to think: who are all the users, what are all the paths they take, and where are the failure points with the highest consequence? Prioritizing by business and compliance impact — not just visual severity — is a product skill, not just a QA skill.

💡 Testing a compliance platform taught me that the most important bugs aren't always the most visible ones — they're the ones that block users from completing what they actually came to do.
